This notice explains what personal data DealAnalysis, Inc. collects through www.dealanalysis.com (the “Site”), how we use it, and the choices you have.

1.Who we are

DealAnalysis, Inc. (“DealAnalysis,” “we,” “us”) is a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 2550 Pacific Avenue, Suite 700, Dallas, Texas 75226, United States, and is the controller of your personal data. Our UK affiliate, DealAnalysis Ltd (London), handles data of United Kingdom and EEA visitors. Contact us at info@dealanalysis.com.

2.What we collect

  • Information you provide: your name, firm or organization, email address, and area of interest when you use our enquiry form, plus anything you send us by email or phone.
  • Information collected automatically: limited, aggregated usage data through analytics — pages viewed, approximate country, device and browser type, and referring source — collected on a cookieless, anonymized basis by default.

We do not ask for sensitive personal information; please do not send it to us.

3.Why we use it

We use personal data to respond to your enquiry and provide what you request; to operate, secure, and improve the Site; and to meet our legal obligations and protect our rights. For UK and EEA visitors, our lawful bases are, respectively, taking steps at your request and our legitimate interests; legitimate interests and (for analytics cookies) your consent; and legal obligation and legitimate interests.

4.Cookies and analytics

By default, our analytics run cookieless: Google Analytics is loaded with consent denied, sending anonymous, aggregated measurement signals without storing identifiers on your device. Non-essential cookies are set only if you accept them through our cookie banner. We use no advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

5.Who we share it with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with: service providers who operate the Site on our instructions (principally Google, for analytics and the spreadsheet and email workflow that receives form submissions, and our hosting provider); our professional advisors; and authorities or other parties where required by law or in connection with a corporate transaction such as a financing or sale.

6.International transfers

We are based in the United States, so personal data from the United Kingdom or EEA is transferred to the U.S. under appropriate safeguards, such as the Standard Contractual Clauses and the UK International Data Transfer Addendum.

7.How long we keep it

We keep enquiry data only as long as needed to respond to you and manage our relationship, plus a reasonable period for legal and business purposes, after which we delete or anonymize it.

8.Security

We use appropriate measures, including encryption in transit (TLS), access controls, and self-hosted Site assets. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

9.Your rights

United Kingdom / EEA (GDPR): you may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, or portability of your data; object to processing; withdraw consent; and lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (in the UK, the Information Commissioner’s Office, ico.org.uk).

Texas (Texas Data Privacy and Security Act) and other U.S. states: you may confirm and access your data, correct it, delete it, obtain a copy, and opt out of targeted advertising, sale, or profiling — we do none of these — and you may appeal a decision on your request.

To exercise any right, email info@dealanalysis.com. We respond within the period required by law and will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

The Site is intended for a business audience and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18 (under 16 in the UK and EEA).
